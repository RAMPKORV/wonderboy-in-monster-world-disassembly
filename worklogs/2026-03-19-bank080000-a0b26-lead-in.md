# 2026-03-19 bank080000 `0x0A0B26-0x0A0C82` lead-in pass

- Tightened the front mixed lead-in between the `0x0A07C6-0x0A0B25` four-tuple band and the later
  fixed-width FF-terminated 6-byte record families.
- Replaced the old monolithic `incbin` at `0x0A0B26-0x0A0C82` with a dedicated source module,
  `src/bank080000_a0b26.asm`.
- The new module source-authors `0x0A0B26-0x0A0C78` as 26 explicit FF-terminated structural records.
  Record sizes stay mixed (`26/21/11/11/6/11/41/31/...`) rather than collapsing into one tuple width,
  so the labels remain structural.
- The front records repeatedly reuse short local-offset/control-like chunks built around bytes such as
  `00 01`, `05 01`, `08 01`, `09 01`, `0C 11`, and `11 FA`, which is enough evidence to stop treating the
  whole prelude as one anonymous patch even though the controlling decoder is still unknown.
- Kept the final 10-byte tail at `0x0A0C79-0x0A0C82` as a tiny `incbin`. It does not end on the same local
  `$FF` cadence as the preceding records, so leaving it grouped is the more conservative byte-identity choice.
